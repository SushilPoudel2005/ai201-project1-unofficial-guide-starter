Source: University of Idaho Student Dining Tips & Hacks (Chartwells / Idaho Eats)

[Review 1]
If you are eating at the Hub, the golden rule is to avoid the 12:00 PM to 12:45 PM rush right after morning classes let out. The lines for the grill and pizza stations get incredibly backed up, and you will waste 20 minutes standing around. If you show up at 11:30 AM or wait until 1:15 PM, you can walk right up and get fresh food immediately.

[Review 2]
Don't let your meal exchanges go to waste at the end of the week! A lot of freshmen don't realize that you can use meal exchanges at Einstein Bros. Bagels or the market variables, but they reset every Sunday morning. If it is Friday or Saturday night and you have leftover exchanges, go stock up on drinks, snacks, or grab a baker's dozen of bagels to store in your dorm room mini-fridge.

[Review 3]
The pizza station at Idaho Eats is decent, but the absolute best hack is asking for a fresh personal pie if they look dried out under the heat lamps. If you ask nicely when it's not super busy, the staff will usually slide a fresh custom one in the oven for you. Also, the grill station has the fastest-moving line if you're in a hurry to get to an afternoon lab.
